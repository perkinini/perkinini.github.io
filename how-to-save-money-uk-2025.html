1. Start With a Brutally Honest Budget

Before you can save a single penny, you need to know exactly where your money is going. Most people underestimate their spending by 20–30% — and that gap is where savings go to die.

How to Build a Budget That Works

  • List every income source — salary, freelance work, benefits, side hustles
  • Categorise your spending — fixed costs (rent, bills, subscriptions) vs. variable costs (food, nights out, clothes)
  • Identify your 'leak categories' — the areas where money disappears without much to show for it

3. Slash Your Subscription Spend

Subscription creep is real. The average UK household is now paying for services they've forgotten they have — old streaming platforms, unused gym memberships, duplicate cloud storage plans.

Quick Subscription Audit Checklist

  • [ ] Streaming services (do you really need Netflix, Disney+, AND Apple TV?)
  • [ ] Gym memberships you don't use
  • [ ] Software subscriptions (Microsoft 365, Adobe, etc.)
  • [ ] Premium app upgrades
  • [ ] Magazine or newspaper subscriptions
  • [ ] Insurance extras you've auto-renewed without checking

Cancel everything you haven't used in the last 30 days. You can always resubscribe if you miss it — you almost certainly won't.

5. Cut Your Energy Bills (Yes, There's Still Room)

Energy prices in 2025 remain above pre-2022 levels for most households. Even with the Ofgem price cap, there's still meaningful money to be saved through smart usage.

  • Switch tariffs or suppliers — use comparison sites like Uswitch or MoneySuperMarket
  • Install a smart meter if you haven't already — it makes overconsumption visible and changes behaviour
  • Check your boiler settings — most boilers are set too high by default
  • Loft and cavity wall insulation grants — check if you qualify under the Great British Insulation Scheme

6. Reduce Your Food Shopping Bill

Groceries are one of the fastest-growing household expenses — but also one of the most controllable.

Practical Supermarket Saving Tips

  • Switch to own-brand or value ranges for staples like pasta, rice, tinned goods, and cleaning products — quality is often comparable
  • Use cashback apps like Shopmium or Checkout Smart for branded goods you already buy
  • Plan meals weekly before shopping — it dramatically reduces waste and impulse buys
  • Shop at discount supermarkets like Aldi or Lidl for your main shop, and use Tesco or Sainsbury's for top-ups and loyalty points
  • Use the Too Good To Go app for cheap surplus food from local restaurants and cafés

7. Tackle Your Debt Strategically

Debt is the silent killer of savings potential. If you're paying 20%+ interest on a credit card while earning 5% on savings, the maths simply doesn't work in your favour.

Prioritise paying off high-interest debt before building large savings (beyond a small emergency fund of £1,000). Once consumer debt is cleared, redirect those monthly payments straight into savings.

For free, impartial debt advice in the UK, contact StepChange or the Money and Pensions Service (MaPS) — both offer genuinely excellent guidance at no cost.

8. Claim Every Benefit and Tax Relief You're Entitled To

Billions of pounds in UK benefits and tax reliefs go unclaimed every year. Don't leave money on the table.

  • Marriage Allowance — worth up to £252/year for eligible couples
  • Working from home tax relief — claim via HMRC if you work from home even part of the week
  • Child Benefit — review eligibility if your circumstances have changed
  • Free childcare hours — expanded in 2024/25 for children aged 9 months+
  • Council Tax discounts — single occupancy, students, and low-income households may qualify

Use the government's benefits calculator at gov.uk to check what you might be missing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I save each month in the UK in 2025?

A popular rule of thumb is the 50/30/20 rule — 50% of take-home pay on needs, 30% on wants, and 20% on savings and debt repayment. In practice, even saving 5–10% consistently is a strong foundation. Start where you are and increase it gradually.

Is it worth using a savings app like Chip in 2025?

Absolutely, especially if you struggle to save manually. Chip automates the process and removes the temptation to spend. Combined with a competitive interest rate on your saved balance, it's one of the easiest wins available to UK savers right now.

Should I invest or save in 2025?

Ideally, both — but in the right order. First, build a cash emergency fund of 3–6 months' expenses in an easy-access account. Then, consider investing surplus funds for goals that are 5+ years away. A platform like Freetrade makes it straightforward to start investing within a tax-efficient ISA wrapper.

How does the UK ISA allowance work in 2025?

Every UK adult gets an annual ISA allowance of £20,000 in the 2025/26 tax year. You can split this across different ISA types (Cash ISA, Stocks & Shares ISA, Innovative Finance ISA). Any growth or interest within the ISA is completely free from income tax and capital gains tax.

What's the fastest way to save money in the UK right now?

The fastest wins are usually: cancelling unused subscriptions, switching energy tariffs, automating savings on payday, and claiming any tax relief or benefits you're owed. Combined, these steps can free up hundreds of pounds a month with relatively little effort.
